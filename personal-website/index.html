Terragy: Reimagining Airport Energy

Project Overview

The airport industry moves people and goods globally, but aviation emissions heavily impact the environment. Jet fuel combustion releases greenhouse gases, notably carbon dioxide, which traps heat and warms the planet. While carbon dioxide supports plant life, at cruising altitudes there are no plants to absorb it, compounding the warming effect.

Terragy is a research and product initiative I created to help airports repay their environmental footprint by harvesting energy from existing airflows. The concept focuses on a self-sufficient turbine system designed to generate electricity with a minimal lifecycle carbon footprint. The core idea is to reimagine how to use Earth's energy to power critical airport systems.

Product Purpose and Application

The primary target is runway and airfield lighting: a turbine-based system harvesting the airflow generated by aircraft movements could offset a portion of the electrical load. Only a small fraction of airports are fully renewable today; many still rely on grid electricity with significant carbon intensity. This is where Terragy and its turbine concept (Plane.Aero) can provide clean, on-site power contributions.

Environmental and Economic Impact

Terragy aims to create meaningful change across multiple dimensions:

  • Reduce the carbon footprint of airport operations
  • Provide a renewable electricity source for airfield lighting
  • Help airports save on electricity costs over time
  • Create opportunities for green jobs and education on sustainability
  • Support alignment with United Nations Sustainable Development Goals

Real-World Context

For example, Heathrow has initiatives to reduce emissions, including the use of LED lights. Typical LED airfield fixtures can be on the order of dozens of watts per unit, and Terragy's concept is to contribute to that load through localized generation. Exact power figures depend on light type, location, and operating conditions; the goal is to contribute meaningful energy where airflow is present.

Technical Direction and Validation

  • Iterative prototyping and CFD-informed design to optimize turbine geometry for runway-adjacent environments
  • Exploration of placement options, safety constraints, and maintenance access
  • Concept-level energy yield estimation based on traffic patterns and local wind profiles
  • Validation: Demonstrate measurable reductions in net electricity consumption for lighting and document performance data to assess carbon and cost benefits over time

Key Goals

Environmental Impact

Reduce airport carbon footprint through renewable energy generation

Technical Innovation

Develop efficient turbine systems optimized for airport environments

Economic Benefit

Provide cost-effective renewable energy solutions for airports

STEM.3: Transforming STEM Education

Initiative Overview

STEM.3 is an educational initiative I founded to make Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics more accessible, engaging, and relevant. It treats STEM as a way to understand and improve the world through curiosity, creativity, and problem-solving.

Motivation and Vision

Many students lose interest when STEM is taught purely as theory. Real innovation begins when learning connects to real life. STEM.3 reimagines the experience through hands-on exploration, real-world engineering links, and a focus on technology and sustainability.

The project addresses educational inequality by creating resources specifically for underprivileged KS3 students in Wales, ensuring that economic barriers don't limit access to quality STEM education.

Approach and Impact

STEM.3 aims to inspire curiosity and confidence through experiments, projects, and discussions that help learners see themselves as creators. It advocates for education that prepares students not only for exams but for life, developing critical thinking, responsibility, and imaginative problem-solving that fuels the next generation of engineers and scientists.

The initiative focuses on making STEM feel connected to real-world challenges, showing students how their learning can contribute to solving global problems and improving society.

Educational Philosophy

STEM.3 matters because it represents what education should be: not just preparation for exams, but preparation for life. The initiative nurtures young minds to think critically, act responsibly, and imagine fearlessly, creating the foundation for future innovators and problem-solvers.

Core Principles

Accessibility

Making quality STEM education available to all students, regardless of background

Engagement

Connecting theoretical concepts to real-world applications and challenges

Innovation

Encouraging creative problem-solving and scientific thinking
